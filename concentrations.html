Optional Concerntrations

University College students who major in Psychology may select from three optional concentrations.

Lifespan Development:

Provides an understanding of the cognitive and physiological changes that occur over the lifespan, including childhood, adolescence, and older adulthood. This concentration provides a foundation for work in psychology, social work, education, and other human services.

    Required Courses (3 units)
    • Abnormal Psychology (U09 354)
    • Perspectives on Counseling (U09 330)

    Elective Courses (3 units)—choose two of the following (others with authorization):
    • Abnormal Child Psychology (U09 3195)
    • Addiction and Treatment (U09 488)
    • Health Psychology (U09 358)

Counseling Foundations:

The application of psychology in the clinical field. This concentration provides a foundation for careers in clinical psychology, social work, counseling, or related fields in mental health services.

    Required Courses (3 units)
    • Developmental Psychology (U09 322)

    Elective Courses (6 units)—choose two of the following (others with authorization):
    • Psychology of Adolescence (U09 325)
    • Psychology of Aging (U09 4261)
    • Psychology of Young Adulthood (new)

Gerontology:

Social, cognitive, and health aspects of aging. This concentration provides a foundation for careers in all healthcare and related service areas for an aging population.

    Required Courses (3 units)
    • Psychology of Aging (U09 4261)

    Elective Courses (6 units)—choose two of the following (others with authorization):
    • Memory & Aging (U09 308)
    • Aging & Health Behavior (new)
    • Social Gerontology (new)

Policies for Optional Concentrations

Each optional concentration requires nine units of course work. A student may pursue only one optional concentration. Courses applied to an optional concentration may not be used to fulfill any other requirement for the major. If two courses completed for an optional concentration fall within the same distribution, then that distribution is satisfied. These optional concentrations are available only to University College students admitted to a Bachelor of Science degree with a major in Psychology.
